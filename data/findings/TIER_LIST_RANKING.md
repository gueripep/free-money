# The Calculus of Outperformance: Official Tier List Ranking
*A multi-factor, cross-sectional evaluation of our curated hyper-growth cohort.*

## S-Tier: The Compounders (Unicorns)
*Companies exhibiting elite capital efficiency, impenetrable moats, massive runways, and an acceptable margin of safety. These are the highest probability multi-baggers.*

*   **I.CO.P. (ICOP.MI):** The absolute quantitative leader of the cohort. ICOP delivers a **3GP score of 222.5**, more than doubling the institutional "elite" threshold. It successfully weaponizes Dimension I (Moat) through specialized trenchless technology, manifesting in a **177.8% EBITDA expansion** and a **44.8% ROI**. With a **€1.1 Billion backlog** providing over five years of revenue visibility and a pristine post-IPO net-cash position, ICOP represents the "perfect" marriage of hyper-growth and structural safety.
*   **GENERALFINANCE (GF.MI):** A mathematical masterpiece in the fintech space. Generalfinance boasts a **3GP score of 186.2** and a **Return on Equity (ROE) of 34.9%**, signaling elite Dimension II capital efficiency. Its Dimension I moat is verified by a **300 bps reduction in Cost/Income ratio** (Operating Leverage) while maintaining **89.7% Gross Margins**. Trading at a **PEG of 0.27**, the market is fundamentally failing to price its localized monopoly in Italian distressed factoring.
*   **FRANCHETTI (FCH.MI):** The premier "Infra-Tech" compounder. Franchetti’s **3GP score of 146.5** and **75.6% Gross Margins** place it in the top 5% of global industrial-tech equities. It dominates Dimension IV (Runway) by capitalizing on the non-discretionary global infrastructure maintenance crisis. While Dimension V (Forensic Risk) identifies high Work-in-Progress (WIP) accruals, the **68% EBITDA growth** and specialized AI-moat justify a Tier-1 weighting.

## A-Tier: High-Probability Contenders
*Excellent businesses that fall slightly short of S-Tier due to valuation premiums, minor margin decay, or slightly higher penetration rates.*

*   **BAIKOWSKI (ALBKK.PA):** Boasts an S-Tier **3GP score of 115** and exceptional **990 bps operating margin expansion**. However, it is demoted from S-Tier due to a massive Dimension IV forensic penalty: **71% customer concentration** among its top 10 clients. While its role in the semiconductor and EV battery supply chain is vital, this "one-trick pony" risk creates a fragility that prevents unconstrained capital allocation.
*   **GOODTECH (GOD.OL):** A textbook "Special Situation" winner. After shedding value-destructive subsidiaries, the core "Continuing Operations" exhibit a **72% Gross Margin** and a potential **3GP of 96.7**. Its **EV/GP of 0.51x** is the cheapest in the cohort relative to a **57% order backlog growth**. It misses S-Tier only because the Dimension II ROIIC is still recovering from a deep restructuring phase.
*   **ELES (ELES.MI):** The highest quality-of-earnings profile in the group. With an **OCF/NI ratio of 5x** and a net-cash balance sheet, ELES is forensically "bulletproof." Its **66% Gross Margins** prove moat durability, but it is held back from S-Tier by stagnant near-term revenue growth. It is a "coiled spring" awaiting a Dimension IV market inflection in AI-testing.

## B-Tier: The "Show Me" Story (Hares)
*Companies with high top-line growth but structural flaws in capital efficiency (e.g., burning cash to grow, poor ROIC, low gross margins). They have potential but unacceptable risk-adjusted profiles currently.*

*   **ABEO (ABEO.PA):** While possessing a strong Dimension I moat (62% margins, Olympic pedigree), ABEO is hindered by a low **3GP score of 46.3** and high relative leverage (**118% D/E**). It is a "Value-Ant" rather than a "Hyper-Growth" engine. The capital efficiency is hampered by legacy M&A goodwill, requiring a multi-year integration period before attaining S-Tier status.
*   **VALUE8 (VALUE.AS):** Trading at a massive **38% discount to NAV** with a fortress balance sheet (93% solvency), the valuation is enticing. However, it receives a terminal Dimension V penalty due to a **Qualified Audit Opinion**. In our ranking metaplan, forensic accounting red flags trigger an automatic demotion regardless of underlying asset quality.
*   **GLINT (GLINT.LS):** A high-quality defensive business with an **8.5% FCF yield** and elite cash conversion. However, a **3GP score of 9.4** and negative revenue growth disqualify it from the "Hyper-Growth" category. It has saturated its domestic SAM (Portugal) and must prove its Dimension IV expansion into Spain before moving up the hierarchy.

## C-Tier / F-Tier: The Value Traps & Disqualifications
*Companies that failed the forensic downside risk assessment (Altman Z-Score flags, severe accruals) or have completely saturated their TAM.*

*   **GAUMONT (GAM.PA):** A Dimension II disaster. With a **3GP score of -109.4** and a **31.8% revenue contraction**, Gaumont is an "Asset Play" in terminal decline. While the IP library has value, the current ROIIC proves management is destroying capital in the US production market.
*   **DIGITAL BROS (DIB.MI):** Disqualified on Dimension V (Forensic Risk). A **Current Ratio of 0.51** indicates an impending liquidity crisis. With a **3GP score of -26.1** and massive asset impairments, the company is a "Grasshopper" that has exhausted its cash runway and lost its "Right to Grow."

Capital allocation should be heavily skewed toward **I.CO.P (ICOP.MI)** and **Generalfinance (GF.MI)**. These two entities represent the rare "Mathematical Ideal" where Dimension II (Capital Efficiency) and Dimension III (Growth-Adjusted Valuation) converge. ICOP’s massive backlog provides a floor for forward compounding that is unique in the micro-cap universe, while Generalfinance’s PEG of 0.27 offers the cohort's most significant "mispricing-to-velocity" ratio.

Investors seeking asymmetric "Deep Value" should look to **Goodtech (GOD.OL)**, where the 0.5x EV/GP multiple provides a massive margin of safety against the current backlog expansion. Avoid **Digital Bros** and **Gaumont** entirely; their negative 3GP scores act as a "gravity well" that will likely lead to further capital impairment despite optically "cheap" asset-based valuations. The focus must remain on companies where operating leverage is active and the "3GP" engine is firing above 100.
